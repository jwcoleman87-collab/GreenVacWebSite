How it works

Water in, slurry out. That's the trick.

1

High-pressure water breaks up the ground

A hand-held wand, not a bucket. It cuts through clay and compacted fill but won't cut poly, copper, conduit or roots.

2

The vacuum pulls the slurry into the tank

The trailer sucks the wet spoil straight up the hose. No spoil piles all over the yard unless you want them.

3

Clean hole, everything still attached

You're left with a neat excavation and whatever was buried sitting there untouched. No teeth, no blades, nothing that can snag a cable.

Do I need Dial Before You Dig first?

Yes — it's free and it's the law for most jobs. James works off the plans, then the hydrovac proves what's actually down there, because the plans and the ground don't always agree.

Can the trailer get to a backyard job?

Usually. The rig stays on the driveway or street and the hoses come to the dig — that's the point of a compact setup. Tight side access is most of the work he does. A photo of the access with your enquiry helps a lot.

What if the job turns out bigger than it looked?

James tells you before it becomes a bill. The estimate is a starting point, and any change gets talked about on site first — no surprise line items.

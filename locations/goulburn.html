Older Infrastructure — Goulburn's Underground Challenge

Goulburn was established in 1833 — making it one of Australia's oldest inland cities. Its established residential areas include properties with utility infrastructure installed across vastly different eras. Stormwater systems, gas reticulation, and water services in older parts of the city may predate any modern asset management system, and Dial Before You Dig records in these areas are often incomplete or inaccurate.

The industrial areas around Goulburn — including the Hume Highway commercial corridor and the industrial estate — have their own infrastructure challenges: heavy infrastructure, multiple service corridors, and in some cases, decommissioned services that still appear on plans but are no longer in their recorded positions.

In both contexts, mechanical excavation near services is a calculated risk that non-destructive digging eliminates. The hydrovac physically reveals what's underground before the mechanical crew advances — protecting both the infrastructure and the contractor from the consequences of an unexpected strike.

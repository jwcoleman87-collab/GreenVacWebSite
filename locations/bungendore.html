Hydro Excavation in Bungendore

Bungendore sits on the Kings Highway between Canberra and Braidwood — GreenVac's home base — placing it squarely in the centre of the operating area. At under 30 minutes' drive from Braidwood, Bungendore is one of the locations GreenVac reaches fastest, and it's a town whose particular mix of property types creates consistent demand for careful, non-destructive excavation.

The town has experienced strong residential growth driven by its proximity to Canberra and its rural lifestyle appeal. New subdivisions have filled in around the older town core, and rural-residential lifestyle blocks on the town's fringe continue to attract buyers from the ACT. This growth has created a landscape where new infrastructure and old infrastructure sit in close proximity — and where the documentation of underground services is often incomplete in both directions.

The Challenge of Mixed Infrastructure in Bungendore

Older Bungendore properties — particularly those in the original town core and on properties carved from historic rural lots — often have water, drainage, and electrical services with no current asset records. Private water supplies, old stock water systems, and drainage works completed before the age of digital record-keeping may not appear on any Dial Before You Dig plan.

Newer estates in Bungendore present a different but related problem. Service installations during estate construction are sometimes recorded inaccurately, and private utilities — irrigation, NBN lead-ins, gas connections — are installed without comprehensive documentation. The result is that even a property built ten years ago may have underground services in places no plan acknowledges.

Non-destructive digging resolves both scenarios. Whether the concern is aged infrastructure with no records or new infrastructure with incomplete records, the hydrovac physically reveals what's underground without the risk of mechanical contact.

Services Available in Bungendore

GreenVac provides the full range of hydrovac services across Bungendore and the surrounding Palerang area:

  • Non-Destructive Digging (NDD) — safe exposure of gas, water, electrical, and telecommunications services before any mechanical works
  • Utility Potholing — precise verification of service depth and position at specific points before trenching or building
  • Trenching — clean hydrovac trenches for new pipe, cable, and irrigation installation
  • Leak Detection Support — precise excavation around suspected leak points without damaging garden beds, paving, or lawns
  • Pit & Cattle Grid Cleaning — clearing service pits and rural infrastructure on lifestyle properties
  • Tight Access Excavation — residential backyards, narrow side passages, and sites where full-size rigs can't operate

Lifestyle and Rural-Residential Properties

Bungendore's fringe is characterised by rural-residential and lifestyle properties — blocks ranging from one to fifty hectares, with a mix of residential buildings, rural infrastructure, and hobby farm operations. Many of these properties have private water supplies, sheds, and outbuildings that have accumulated services over decades, often with no systematic records.

GreenVac's compact trailer rig is well suited to these conditions. It navigates unsealed access roads, fits through property gates, and can reach working areas well away from the main road without the logistics complications that come with a large truck-mounted rig.

Bungendore's rapid residential growth has created a situation where new subdivisions sit alongside older rural lots with poorly documented utility infrastructure. Physical verification by potholing or NDD is the reliable approach before breaking ground.
